Doctor Who: 8th Doctor Adventures

Time of the Revolution

"Ok Charley, I think we should find a newspaper, that would be the easiest way to find out where we are without causing a possible disturbance"

"Knowing you there will still BE a disturbance" The pair walk a bit before coming across a printing press for The Pennsylvania Evening Post.

"Charley, you wait out here. I don't have any currency on me so I will have to talk my way into getting a paper"

"We could just ask for the date you know Doctor..."

"Absolutely right Charley! Although I would love to see where in American history we are. I know a lot about the beginnings of this country but not all the nitty gritty dates"

"We don't even know if this is America yet Doctor!",

"Why of course it is Charley, if I know my geographical smells well enough we are in Boston! Now just wait out here. I will only be a moment.",

"If I get kidnapped again it is your fault you know"

"Har har, Charley" The Doctor walks into the shop and from the windows on the building's exterior it is easy to tell that the Doctor is getting into a confrontation. Charley however does as the Doctor asked and is waiting outside.

"Hey you, girl" A voice said from behind Charley,

"Me?" Charley said, turning suddenly. In front of her was a tall man with messy hair and stubble on his face.

"Yes, you girly. You one of them loyalists?"

"The what?"

"Don't play dumb with me, you are standing outside of a loyalist publication, are you a loyalist?" Before Charley could answer the man the door swung open with the Doctor rushing out

"You can leave her alone now. We are not loyalist to the British Pigs, we just don't happen to be from around here"

"Pompous accent there, y'must be British"

"Like I said, we aren't from around here. Your accent isn't that different to begin with. I suggest you leave me and my traveling companion alone. We are not on the side of the British army." The man turned away and left in a huff.

"British pigs? What is going on Doctor?"

"We have come at a very interesting time Charley. 1778, the American Revolution is in full swing, and it just so happens that The Pennsylvania Evening Post begins publishing loyalist views, despite it being the first english language publication of the Declaration of Independence"

"Ok, fine. Now that you know the date can we leave here?"

"Of course not! I've always wanted to meet Thomas Paine, or visit the Continental Congress! Come on Charley, we won't have time for both!" The Doctor starts running off while Charley just stands there not shocked in the slightest.
