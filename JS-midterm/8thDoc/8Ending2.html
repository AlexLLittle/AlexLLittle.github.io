Time of the Revolution

“You know what Doctor, that was actually a fun trip!” Charley said to the Doctor while entering onto the TARDIS.

“I knew that if we walked around a bit we would find something for you to do in Philadelphia!”

“Fairmount park was beautiful, and the market had such good fruit!”

“Don’t forget about seeing a colonial performance of Hamilton!”

“Shame you had to erase their memories of it”

“Indeed, but the ending does spoil their history, well, most of it does not just the ending” The Doctor begins to fiddle with the console and is about to take off, “Singapore 1930?”

“Yes Doctor, but first I have something I want to ask you about.”

“What is it Charely? It isn’t about Edith or that fact that you are supposed to be dead is it?”

“No, of course not! When you were talking to Thomas, you said something odd about being a warrior?”

“Oh yes, I suppose I did”

“What did you mean?”

“Well, as a Timelord I regenerate and my previous face was, mischievous to say the least.

“Is that all?”

“Well for now I suppose. My people have many prophecies and one of them describes a war to end all wars. I fear it is soon.

“How soon?” The Doctor doesn’t answer Charley, pretending not to hear her, “Doctor, how soon?”

“I don’t know!” The Doctor yells, Charley backs up, she had never seen him blow up like that before, “I’m sorry Charley. I shouldn’t have yelled at you. I just worry about the future. My future” the only noise in the room is the TARDIS itself, both of its occupants are dead silent, “let's get to Singapore”

The End
